EU AI Act enters full enforcement on 2 August 2026 — GPAI supervision, transparency obligations, and €35 million penalties go live in 25 days

The EU AI Act reaches full application on 2 August 2026 — now 25 days away — activating enforcement powers that sit on top of the GPAI (general-purpose AI model) obligations already in force since August 2025. From 2 August, the European AI Office gains full supervisory authority over frontier AI providers; transparency and disclosure obligations for AI-generated content become enforceable across the EU; and fines of up to €35 million or 7% of global annual turnover become applicable for the most serious violations. The high-risk AI compliance deadline for Annex III systems was extended to December 2027 under the Digital Omnibus package, but this does not affect the transparency, prohibited practices, or GPAI obligations, which are unchanged and enforceable from August 2.

Why it matters: The August 2 deadline is not deferred — only high-risk AI obligations were pushed under the Digital Omnibus. Organisations producing AI-generated content for EU audiences, deploying general-purpose AI models, or operating AI systems that interact with users (chatbots, recommendation systems) need to confirm their transparency and disclosure practices are enforcement-ready before the end of this month. The €35M/7% fine structure is the highest risk tier in the Act and applies to prohibited practices and non-compliant GPAI models. Any organisation that has not mapped its AI systems to the Act's categories as of today is behind schedule; the window to remediate before enforcement is weeks, not months.
