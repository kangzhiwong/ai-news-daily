Anthropic warns of recursive self-improvement; Claude wrote 80% of its own production code in May

In "When AI Builds Itself" (4 Jun 2026), Anthropic disclosed that over 80% of code merged into its production codebase in May 2026 was authored by Claude — up from low single digits when Claude Code launched in early 2025. Why it matters: If frontier models are now substantively building themselves, two things shift quickly: AI tooling roadmaps compress, and governance questions about "how do you audit an AI that wrote itself?" enter the mainstream. Colorado AI Act enforcement begins 30 June 2026

Colorado's AI Act took effect 1 February 2026 with a grace period; enforcement begins 30 June. Covered businesses deploying "high-risk" AI for consequential decisions (employment, housing, healthcare, insurance, government services, etc.) must run risk-management programs, consumer disclosures and algorithmic-discrimination mitigation. California's automated-decision-technology rules follow on 1 January 2027.

Why it matters: Organisations with multi-state US footprints will increasingly need to produce impact assessments and discrimination-testing artefacts on request. Templating these now (before they're asked for) is cheap insurance.
