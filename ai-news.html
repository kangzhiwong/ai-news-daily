Illinois Governor Pritzker signs landmark AI safety bill — first US state to mandate annual third-party AI audits, $1–3 million penalties

Illinois Governor JB Pritzker signed landmark AI regulation legislation on 6 July 2026, making Illinois the first US state to require mandatory annual third-party safety audits for companies deploying covered AI systems. The law is modelled on similar bills in California and New York and introduces civil penalties of up to $1 million for a first violation and $3 million for subsequent violations, enforceable by the state attorney general. The bill covers companies operating large AI systems that materially affect Illinois residents and takes effect on 1 January 2028.

Why it matters: The mandatory annual third-party audit requirement is a structural escalation in state-level AI regulation: prior US state laws focused on documentation, impact assessments, and disclosure obligations. Illinois is the first to create a recurring independent audit obligation backed by million-dollar penalties. For organisations deploying AI systems that reach Illinois residents, the 2028 effective date provides an 18-month runway — but building the documentation, vendor records, and AI system inventories needed to support an external audit should begin now. The law also sets a legislative template other states are likely to follow, given Illinois's track record as a policy bellwether in data protection (BIPA) and employment law.

EU AI Act enters full enforcement on 2 August 2026 — GPAI supervision, transparency obligations, and €35 million penalties go live in 24 days

The EU AI Act reaches full application on 2 August 2026 — now 24 days away — activating enforcement powers that sit on top of the GPAI (general-purpose AI model) obligations already in force since August 2025. From 2 August, the European AI Office gains full supervisory authority over frontier AI providers; transparency and disclosure obligations for AI-generated content become enforceable across the EU; and fines of up to €35 million or 7% of global annual turnover become applicable for the most serious violations. The high-risk AI compliance deadline for Annex III systems was extended to December 2027 under the Digital Omnibus package, but this does not affect the transparency, prohibited practices, or GPAI obligations, which are unchanged and enforceable from August 2.

Why it matters: The August 2 deadline is not deferred — only high-risk AI obligations were pushed under the Digital Omnibus. Organisations producing AI-generated content for EU audiences, deploying general-purpose AI models, or operating AI systems that interact with users (chatbots, recommendation systems) need to confirm their transparency and disclosure practices are enforcement-ready before the end of this month. The €35M/7% fine structure is the highest risk tier in the Act and applies to prohibited practices and non-compliant GPAI models. Any organisation that has not mapped its AI systems to the Act's categories as of today is behind schedule; the window to remediate before enforcement is weeks, not months.
