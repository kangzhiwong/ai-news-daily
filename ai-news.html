Illinois Governor Pritzker signs landmark AI safety bill — first US state to mandate annual third-party AI audits, $1–3 million penalties

Illinois Governor JB Pritzker signed the Artificial Intelligence Safety Measures Act into law on 6 July 2026, making Illinois the first US state to require mandatory annual third-party safety audits for companies deploying covered AI systems. The law covers large AI developers — those with more than $500 million in annual gross revenue — and requires them to publish explanations of how their products could pose a "catastrophic risk," disclose how they identify and respond to critical safety incidents, and report critical safety incidents to the state within 72 hours. Civil penalties reach $1 million for a first violation and $3 million for subsequent violations, enforceable by the state attorney general. The bill passed with broad bipartisan support — unanimously in the House — and the core audit and safety-framework requirements take effect 1 January 2028. Both OpenAI and Anthropic publicly supported the legislation.

Why it matters: The mandatory annual third-party audit requirement is a structural escalation in state-level AI regulation: prior US state laws focused on documentation, impact assessments, and disclosure obligations. Illinois is the first to create a recurring independent audit obligation backed by million-dollar penalties. For organisations deploying AI systems that reach Illinois residents, the 2028 effective date for core requirements provides an 18-month runway — but building the documentation, vendor records, and AI system inventories needed to support an external audit should begin now. The broad bipartisan passage and the explicit support from the two largest US AI labs signals that mandatory AI auditing is consolidating as a durable legislative template, with other states expected to follow.
