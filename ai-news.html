AI Governance & Regulation

Trump signs AI executive order seeking voluntary 30-day pre-release access to frontier models

On 2 June 2026, President Trump signed "Promoting Advanced Artificial Intelligence Innovation and Security," asking AI developers to voluntarily share new frontier models with the US government for up to 30 days before wider release for security evaluation and benchmarking. The framework is non-compulsory — developers cannot be forced to participate — but Anthropic, OpenAI, and Google have indicated cooperation. Federal agencies must also develop a classified benchmarking process for assessing the advanced cyber capabilities of new AI systems within 60 days.

Why it matters: The US is establishing a pre-deployment evaluation channel for the most capable AI systems — a lightweight, voluntary form of the pre-market review framework that regulators in other jurisdictions have proposed as mandatory. Worth tracking as a leading indicator of how sovereign AI evaluation frameworks evolve, and whether voluntary participation eventually hardens into an informal or formal expectation.

Bipartisan Great American AI Act proposes 3-year federal preemption of US state AI development laws

Reps. Jay Obernolte and Lori Trahan released a 269-page bipartisan discussion draft on 4 June 2026 proposing the first comprehensive US federal AI framework. The headline provision: a three-year preemption of state laws specifically regulating frontier AI model development — freezing rules in California, Colorado, and Illinois among others — while existing consumer-protection, civil-rights, and privacy laws remain intact. Large AI developers would face mandatory semi-annual third-party audits. Notably, Colorado's original AI Act enforcement was already stayed by a federal court in April 2026 after a DOJ intervention; Colorado's governor subsequently signed a replacement law (SB 26-189) effective 1 January 2027.

Why it matters: A federal US framework would simplify multi-state compliance planning significantly — but the preemption sunset and eventual federal floor may land at a lower bar than existing state laws set. Still a discussion draft with an uncertain legislative pathway; the direction of federal intent is now documented, and organisations with multi-state US AI deployments should track its progress closely.
