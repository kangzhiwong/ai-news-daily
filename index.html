Illinois Governor Pritzker signs landmark AI safety bill — first US state to mandate annual third-party AI audits, $1–3 million penalties

Illinois Governor JB Pritzker signed the Artificial Intelligence Safety Measures Act into law on 6 July 2026, making Illinois the first US state to require mandatory annual third-party safety audits for companies deploying covered AI systems. The law is modelled on similar bills in California and New York (each signed in late 2025) and introduces civil penalties of up to $1 million for a first violation and $3 million for subsequent violations, enforceable by the state attorney general. The bill covers companies operating large AI systems — those generating more than $500 million in annual revenue and trained on massive computing power — that materially affect Illinois residents, and takes effect on 1 January 2028. Together, Illinois, California, and New York represent approximately 40% of the US AI market.

Why it matters: The mandatory annual third-party audit requirement is a structural escalation in state-level AI regulation: prior US state laws focused on documentation, impact assessments, and disclosure obligations. Illinois is the first to create a recurring independent audit obligation backed by million-dollar penalties. For organisations deploying AI systems that reach Illinois residents, the 2028 effective date provides an 18-month runway — but building the documentation, vendor records, and AI system inventories needed to support an external audit should begin now. The combined 40% US AI market coverage of the three-state bloc effectively creates a de facto national standard in the absence of federal regulation, with other states expected to follow the legislative template.

The EU AI Act reaches full application on 2 August 2026 — now 23 days away — activating enforcement powers that sit on top of the GPAI (general-purpose AI model) obligations already in force since August 2025. Organisations producing AI-generated content for EU audiences, deploying general-purpose AI models, or operating AI systems that interact with users (chatbots, recommendation systems) need to confirm their transparency and disclosure practices are enforcement-ready before the end of this month. The €35M/7% fine structure is the highest risk tier in the Act and applies to prohibited practices and non-compliant GPAI models.
